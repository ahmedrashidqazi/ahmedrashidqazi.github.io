**Objective:** Redesign the personal website `ahmedrashidqazi.github.io` to project the image of a "top-tier engineer." This is a complete structural and aesthetic overhaul, not just a theme update. The new design should be modern, professional, and interactive, showcasing technical expertise and research accomplishments.

**I. Overall Aesthetic & Tone**

*   **Theme:** Professional, clean, and modern with a "dark mode" aesthetic. Use a dark background (e.g., `#111827` or a dark slate gray) with contrasting text (e.g., `#E5E7EB` or a light gray).
*   **Accent Color:** A single, striking accent color (e.g., a vibrant blue, green, or orange like `#38BDF8`) for links, buttons, and highlights.
*   **Typography:**
    *   **Headings:** A clean, modern sans-serif font like Inter, Manrope, or a slightly stylized one like Calistoga.
    *   **Body:** A highly readable sans-serif font like Inter or a monospace font like Fira Code or JetBrains Mono to give a technical feel.
*   **Tone:** The language should be concise, confident, and impactful.

**II. Layout & Structure (Single-Page Application Feel)**

The site should be a single, scrollable page with a fixed navigation bar that smoothly scrolls to the corresponding sections.

*   **Navigation Bar (Header):**
    *   **Left:** A simple logo or the name "Ahmed Qazi".
    *   **Right:** Navigation links: `01. About`, `02. Experience`, `03. Projects`, `04. Publications`, `05. Contact`. The numbers give it a deliberate, ordered feel.
    *   **Behavior:** The header should be sticky or become visible on scrolling up. The active section link should be highlighted.

*   **Section Order:**
    1.  Hero Section
    2.  About Section
    3.  Experience Section
    4.  Projects Section
    5.  Publications Section
    6.  Contact Section
    7.  Footer

**III. Content Sections Breakdown**

**1. Hero Section**

*   **Content:**
    *   **Greeting:** "Hi, my name is" in the accent color.
    *   **Name:** "Ahmed Qazi." in a large, bold heading.
    *   **Tagline:** "I build intelligent systems." or a similarly impactful, short statement in a slightly smaller heading.
    *   **Introduction:** A 2-3 sentence summary: "I am a Senior AI Engineer specializing in the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning. I design and build production-ready AI platforms that solve real-world problems."
    *   **Call to Action:** A prominent button with the text "View My Work" that links to the Projects section.

**2. About Section (Titled "01. About Me")**

*   **Layout:** Two-column layout.
*   **Left Column:**
    *   A brief, engaging narrative about your professional journey and passion for AI and neuroscience.
*   **Right Column:**
    *   A list of key technologies and skills, categorized for clarity.
    *   **Categories:** Programming Languages, Frameworks & Libraries, Tools & Platforms.
    *   **Example:**
        *   **Programming Languages:** Python, C++, SQL
        *   **Frameworks & Libraries:** PyTorch, TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, FastAPI, Django
        *   **Tools & Platforms:** Docker, Kubernetes, GCP, AWS, Git

**3. Experience Section (Titled "02. Experience")**

*   **Layout:** A vertical timeline format.
*   **For each role:**
    *   **Title & Company:** "Senior AI Engineer @ Osmium Labs" (company name is a link).
    *   **Dates:** "Oct 2025 - Present".
    *   **Description:** 2-3 bullet points detailing key achievements and responsibilities. Start each bullet with a strong action verb.
    *   **Technologies Used:** A list of relevant technologies for that role (e.g., `PyTorch`, `Kubernetes`, `GCP`).

**4. Projects Section (Titled "03. Projects")**

*   **Layout:** A grid of project cards (2-3 per row).
*   **Each Project Card:**
    *   **Title:** Project Name.
    *   **Description:** A short, one-sentence summary.
    *   **Technologies:** A list of technologies used.
    *   **Links:** Icons linking to the **Live Demo**, **GitHub Repository**, and (if applicable) a **Case Study/Blog Post**.
*   **Key Feature: Featured Projects:** Have 2-3 "featured" projects at the top that are larger and have more detail, including a small screenshot or graphic.

**5. Publications Section (Titled "04. Publications")**

*   **Layout:** A clean, academic-style list.
*   **For each publication:**
    *   **Title:** "Title of the Paper".
    *   **Authors:** List of authors with your name in **bold**.
    *   **Journal/Conference:** "Published in *Journal of Neuroscience*, 2024".
    *   **Links:** Buttons or links for "PDF", "Code", and "DOI".

**6. Contact Section (Titled "05. What's Next?")**

*   **Content:**
    *   **Heading:** "Get In Touch".
    *   **Text:** A short, inviting message. "I'm always open to discussing new projects, research collaborations, or interesting opportunities. Feel free to reach out."
    *   **Call to Action:** A prominent button with your email address: `mailto:ahmedrashidqazi@gmail.com`.

**IV. Footer**

*   **Content:**
    *   A simple, centered line with social media links (GitHub, LinkedIn, Google Scholar).
    *   "Designed & Built by Ahmed Qazi".

**V. Key Features & Interactivity**

*   **Scroll Animations:** Subtle fade-in or slide-up animations as the user scrolls down to each section.
*   **Hover Effects:** Links, buttons, and project cards should have clean hover effects (e.g., a slight change in color or a subtle upward shift).
*   **Responsive Design:** The website must be fully responsive and look great on all devices, from mobile phones to desktops.
*   **Performance:** Optimized for fast loading times.
*   **(Optional) Command Palette:** For an extra "top-tier engineer" touch, consider adding a small, clickable terminal icon that opens a command palette (`Ctrl+K` or `Cmd+K`) allowing users to quickly navigate to different sections (e.g., typing `goto projects`).
